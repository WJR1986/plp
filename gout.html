Gout Recipes for Yungy

By Will

Week 1

Sunday Cooking:

Spicy Chickpea and Vegetable Curry (makes 4 portions):

  • Ingredients:
    • 1 tbsp olive oil
    • 1 onion, chopped
    • 2 cloves garlic, minced
    • 1 inch ginger, grated
    • 1 tsp ground cumin
    • 1 tsp ground coriander
    • ½ tsp turmeric powder
    • ½ tsp chili powder (or more, to taste)
    • 1 can (400g) chopped tomatoes
    • 400ml coconut milk
    • 2 carrots, chopped
    • 1 sweet potato, chopped
    • 1 red bell pepper, deseeded and chopped
    • 1 can (400g) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
    • Fresh coriander, chopped (for garnish)
  • Instructions:
    1. Heat the oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add the onion and cook until softened, about 5 minutes.
    2. Add the garlic, ginger, and spices. Cook for 1 minute more, stirring constantly.
    3. Stir in the chopped tomatoes, coconut milk, carrots, sweet potato, and bell pepper. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for 20 minutes, or until the vegetables are tender.
    4. Stir in the chickpeas and cook for a few minutes more.
    5. Garnish with fresh coriander and serve with brown rice.
  • Freezing: After the curry has cooled, divide it into 4 portions. Freeze 2 portions for use in a future week.

Mediterranean Baked Chicken with Lemon and Herbs (makes 4 portions):

  • Ingredients:
    • 4 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
    • 1 tbsp olive oil
    • 1 lemon, thinly sliced
    • 2 sprigs fresh rosemary
    • 2 sprigs fresh thyme
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • 1 red onion, cut into wedges
    • 200g cherry tomatoes
  • Instructions:
    1. Preheat oven to 200°C (180°C fan/gas mark 6).
    2. Place the chicken breasts in a baking dish.
    3. Drizzle with olive oil and top with lemon slices, rosemary, thyme, salt, and pepper.
    4. Scatter the onion wedges and cherry tomatoes around the chicken.
    5. Bake for 25-30 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the vegetables are tender.
  • Freezing: Once the chicken has cooled, divide it into 4 portions. Freeze 2 portions for use in a future week.

Monday:

  • Lunch: Spicy Chickpea and Vegetable Curry with brown rice.
  • Dinner: Mediterranean Baked Chicken with Lemon and Herbs, served with a side of steamed green beans.

Tuesday:

  • Lunch: Leftover Mediterranean Baked Chicken with a side salad.
  • Dinner: Tofu and Vegetable Stir-fry with Noodles
    • Ingredients:
      • 1 tbsp olive oil
      • 1 block firm tofu, pressed and cubed
      • 1 onion, sliced
      • 1 red bell pepper, deseeded and sliced
      • 1 head of broccoli, cut into florets
      • 1 cup sugar snap peas
      • 2 tbsp low-sodium soy sauce
      • 1 tbsp honey (optional)
      • Noodles of your choice (e.g., rice noodles, whole-wheat noodles)
    • Instructions:
      1. Heat the oil in a large wok or frying pan over high heat.
      2. Add the tofu and cook until golden brown on all sides.
      3. Add the onion, bell pepper, and broccoli. Stir-fry for 5 minutes.
      4. Add the sugar snap peas and stir-fry for 2 minutes more.
      5. Stir in the soy sauce and honey (if using).
      6. Cook the noodles according to package directions.
      7. Toss the noodles with the stir-fry and serve immediately.
    • Freezing: This recipe is not for freezing.

Wednesday:

  • Lunch: Greek Yogurt with Berries and Granola.
  • Dinner: Mexican-style Baked Sweet Potatoes with Black Beans and Salsa
    • Ingredients:
      • 4 sweet potatoes
      • 1 tbsp olive oil
      • 1 can (400g) black beans, drained and rinsed
      • Salsa of your choice
      • Low-fat Greek yogurt
      • Fresh coriander, chopped (for garnish)
    • Instructions:
      1. Preheat oven to 200°C (180°C fan/gas mark 6).
      2. Wash the sweet potatoes and prick them all over with a fork.
      3. Rub with olive oil and bake for 45-60 minutes, or until tender.
      4. While the potatoes are baking, warm the black beans in a pan.
      5. Once the potatoes are cooked, cut them open and top with black beans, salsa, Greek yogurt, and fresh coriander.
    • Freezing: This recipe is not for freezing.

Thursday:

  • Lunch: Leftover Mexican-style Baked Sweet Potatoes.
  • Dinner: Tuna and Chickpea Salad with Whole-Wheat Pita Bread
    • Ingredients:
      • 2 cans (160g each) tuna in water or brine, drained
      • 1 can (400g) chickpeas, drained and rinsed
      • 1 red onion, finely chopped
      • 1 cucumber, diced
      • ½ cup cherry tomatoes, halved
      • ¼ cup chopped fresh parsley
      • 3 tbsp olive oil
      • 2 tbsp lemon juice
      • Salt and pepper to taste
      • Whole-wheat pita bread
    • Instructions:
      1. In a large bowl, combine the tuna, chickpeas, onion, cucumber, tomatoes, and parsley.
      2. In a small bowl, whisk together the olive oil, lemon juice, salt, and pepper.
      3. Pour the dressing over the salad and toss to combine.
      4. Serve with whole-wheat pita bread.
    • Freezing: This recipe is not for freezing.

Friday:

  • Lunch: Egg and Avocado Salad with Whole-Wheat Crackers
    • Ingredients:
      • 3 hard-boiled eggs, chopped
      • 1 ripe avocado, mashed
      • 1 tbsp lemon juice
      • Salt and pepper to taste
      • Whole-wheat crackers
    • Instructions:
      1. In a bowl, combine the chopped eggs, mashed avocado, and lemon juice.
      2. Mix well and season with salt and pepper.
      3. Serve with whole-wheat crackers.
    • Freezing: This recipe is not for freezing.
  • Dinner: Homemade Chicken Fajitas with Whole-Wheat Tortillas
    • Ingredients:
      • 200g boneless, skinless chicken breast, sliced
      • 1 tbsp olive oil
      • 1 onion, sliced
      • 1 red bell pepper, deseeded and sliced
      • 1 green bell pepper, deseeded and sliced
      • 1 tsp chili powder
      • 1 tsp cumin
      • ½ tsp paprika
      • Whole-wheat tortillas
      • Toppings of your choice (e.g., salsa, low-fat Greek yogurt, guacamole)
    • Instructions:
      1. Heat the oil in a large pan over medium heat. Add the chicken and cook until browned on all sides.
      2. Add the onion and bell peppers. Cook for 5 minutes more.
      3. Stir in the chili powder, cumin, and paprika. Cook for 1 minute more, stirring constantly.
      4. Warm the tortillas according to package directions.
      5. Fill the tortillas with the chicken and vegetable mixture and your favorite toppings.
    • Freezing: This recipe is not for freezing.

Important notes on freezing:

  • Cooling: Always allow food to cool completely before freezing to prevent condensation and ice crystals.
  • Containers: Use airtight containers or freezer bags to prevent freezer burn.
  • Labeling: Label your containers with the date and contents for easy identification.
  • Thawing: Thaw frozen meals in the refrigerator overnight for safe and even defrosting.

By freezing portions of the Spicy Chickpea and Vegetable Curry and the Mediterranean Baked Chicken, you'll have convenient and gout-friendly meals ready to go for a future week. This saves you time and ensures you always have a healthy option on hand.

Week 2

Sunday Cooking:

Greek Lemon Chicken and Rice Traybake (makes 4 portions):

  • Ingredients:
    • 4 boneless, skinless chicken thighs
    • 1 tbsp olive oil
    • Juice of 1 lemon
    • 1 tsp dried oregano
    • Salt and pepper to taste
    • 1 red onion, cut into wedges
    • 200g cherry tomatoes
    • 1 cup long-grain rice
    • 2 cups chicken stock (low sodium)
  • Instructions:
    1. Preheat oven to 200°C (180°C fan/gas mark 6).
    2. In a large bowl, toss the chicken thighs with olive oil, lemon juice, oregano, salt, and pepper.
    3. Spread the rice in a baking dish. Arrange the chicken thighs, onion wedges, and cherry tomatoes on top.
    4. Pour the chicken stock over the ingredients.
    5. Bake for 35-40 minutes, or until the chicken is cooked through and the rice is tender.
  • Freezing: After the traybake has cooled, divide it into 4 portions. Freeze 2 portions for use in a future week.

  • Lunch: Leftover Spicy Chickpea and Vegetable Curry (from the freezer).
  • Dinner: Greek Lemon Chicken and Rice Traybake.

Tuesday:

  • Lunch: Leftover Greek Lemon Chicken and Rice Traybake.
  • Dinner: Salmon with Roasted Root Vegetables
    • Ingredients:
      • 2 salmon fillets (about 120g each)
      • 1 tbsp olive oil
      • 2 carrots, peeled and chopped
      • 2 parsnips, peeled and chopped
      • 2 potatoes, peeled and chopped
      • 1 tsp dried rosemary
      • Salt and pepper to taste
    • Instructions:
      1. Preheat oven to 200°C (180°C fan/gas mark 6).
      2. Toss the vegetables with olive oil, rosemary, salt, and pepper on a baking tray.
      3. Place the salmon fillets on the same tray.
      4. Roast for 15-20 minutes, or until the salmon is cooked through and the vegetables are tender.
    • Freezing: This recipe is best enjoyed fresh, but you could freeze leftover cooked salmon for a quick meal later (e.g., flaked into a salad).

  • Freezing: This recipe is not for freezing.
  • Dinner: Homemade Vegetable and Feta Pizza with Whole-Wheat Base
    • Ingredients:
      • 2 whole-wheat pizza bases
      • 1 jar (300g) tomato passata
      • 1 tsp dried oregano
      • 1 clove garlic, minced
      • 1 red onion, thinly sliced
      • 1 red bell pepper, deseeded and sliced
      • 1 courgette, thinly sliced
      • 100g mushrooms, sliced
      • 100g crumbled feta cheese
      • Black olives (optional)
    • Instructions:
      1. Preheat oven to 220°C (200°C fan/gas mark 7).
      2. In a small bowl, combine the passata, oregano, and garlic.
      3. Spread the sauce over the pizza bases.
      4. Top with the onion, peppers, courgette, mushrooms, feta cheese, and olives (if using).
      5. Bake for 10-15 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is melted and bubbly.
    • Freezing: While you can freeze pizza, it's often best enjoyed fresh.

Shopping Schedule

Initial Shop (Before Week 1)

  • Long-lasting items:
    • Pantry: Olive oil, spices (cumin, coriander, turmeric, chilli powder, oregano, rosemary), lentils, brown rice, quinoa, wholemeal crackers, wholemeal pitta bread, wholemeal tortillas, tinned tomatoes, coconut milk, chickpeas, black beans, tuna, low-sodium soy sauce, honey (optional), granola.
    • Freezer: Frozen peas, frozen berries (if using).

Shop 1 (Before Week 1)

  • Fresh Produce: Onions, garlic, ginger, carrots, potatoes, broccoli, asparagus, red peppers, lemons, fresh coriander (optional), romaine lettuce (for side salads).
  • Protein: Chicken thighs, chicken breasts, salmon fillets.
  • Dairy: Greek yoghurt, eggs.

Shop 2 (Mid-Week 1)

  • Fresh Produce: Avocado, red onion, cucumber, cherry tomatoes, chives, bananas.

Shop 3 (Before Week 2)

  • Fresh Produce: Carrots, parsnips, potatoes, red onion, cherry tomatoes, courgette, mushrooms, black olives (optional).
  • Protein: Cod fillets, salmon fillets (if freezing extra from Week 1 not done), chicken thighs, ground turkey.
  • Dairy: Feta cheese, low-fat mozzarella (if using).
  • Other: Wholemeal pizza bases (or alternatives).

Ongoing Shopping Schedule

  • Weekly "Big Shop": This is where you stock up on pantry staples, long-lasting produce (onions, potatoes), and any frozen items. Ideally, do this on a day when you have more time and can compare prices.
  • Mid-week "Top-Up Shop": This is for fresh items that don't last as long, like leafy greens, berries, and some vegetables. This helps ensure you have fresh ingredients for salads and snacks throughout the week.
  • Consider online shopping: If your schedule is very busy, online shopping can be a time-saver. Many supermarkets offer convenient delivery slots.

Tips for Efficient Shopping

  • Meal plan first: Always create your meal plan before you shop to avoid impulse buys and ensure you have everything you need.
  • Check your cupboards: Take stock of what you already have before heading to the store to avoid duplicates.
  • Shop with a list: A list helps you stay focused and avoid unnecessary purchases.
  • Look for deals: Check for weekly specials and discounts, especially on produce and protein.
  • Consider frozen: Frozen fruits and vegetables can be just as nutritious as fresh and are often more affordable.

By following this schedule and using these tips, you can keep your kitchen stocked with fresh, healthy ingredients while minimising trips to the store and maximising your budget.
